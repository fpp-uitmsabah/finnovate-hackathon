Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about the FIN-novate Hackathon 2025.

Who can participate in the hackathon? ‚ñº

The FIN-novate Hackathon is open to all FIN333 students at UiTM. Teams must consist of 5-6 members.

What AI tools will be covered in the workshop? ‚ñº

The AI Accelerator Workshop covers three modules: Analysis (using Gemini for research), Design (using Canva Code and image generators like Nano Banana), and Ethics (AI disclosure and responsible usage).

How will solutions be evaluated? ‚ñº

Solutions are evaluated based on two main components: Infographic Report (50 points) covering project execution, impact, leadership, teamwork, and report quality; and Presentation (50 points) covering topic coverage, fluency, audiovisuals, and defense ability.

What format should the final deliverables be in? ‚ñº

Teams must submit: (1) A 30-slide infographic report in PDF format, and (2) A 10-minute pitch video explaining their solution.

Can we use AI tools for our solution development? ‚ñº

Yes! The hackathon encourages the use of AI tools for analysis, design, and research. However, teams must follow ethical guidelines and include an AI disclosure statement in their submissions.

What happens if our solution wins? ‚ñº

Winning teams receive cash prizes, certificates, and recognition. The top solution may be implemented campus-wide and featured in UiTM publications. All winners will present their solutions at an awards ceremony.
