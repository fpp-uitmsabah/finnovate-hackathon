Individual Stocks
  • What is it? Buying shares of individual companies (e.g., Maybank, Petronas)
  • Control You choose exactly which companies to invest in
  • Diversification You must build diversity yourself (harder)
  • Research Required High - need to analyze each company
  • Minimum Investment One lot = 100 shares (varies by price)
  • Fees Brokerage fees (0.1-0.4% per trade)
  • Risk Level High - concentrated in few companies
  • Best For Active investors who enjoy research
ðŸŽ¯
Unit Trust / Mutual Funds
  • What is it? Pool of money from many investors, managed professionally
  • Control Professional managers make investment decisions
  • Diversification Built-in - spreads across many companies/assets
  • Research Required Low - managers handle the research
  • Minimum Investment As low as RM 100 (very accessible)
  • Fees Management fees (0.5-2% annually) + sales charge
  • Risk Level Lower - diversified across many holdings
  • Best For Beginners and hands-off investors

When to Choose Stocks

  • You enjoy researching companies
  • You have time to monitor investments
  • You want direct control over holdings
  • You're comfortable with higher risk
  • You have capital for diversification (RM 10k+)

When to Choose Unit Trusts

  • You're new to investing
  • You have limited time for research
  • You want professional management
  • You're starting with small amounts
  • You prefer built-in diversification
Scenario 1: Fresh Graduate - RM 5,000 to Invest
Sarah just graduated and has RM 5,000 in savings. She wants to start investing but has limited knowledge and doesn't have much time to research due to her new job.

ðŸ“ˆ Individual Stocks - Not Ideal

With RM 5,000, Sarah can only buy 1-2 stocks (limited diversification). She lacks time to research and monitor companies. High risk of loss if one company performs poorly.

ðŸŽ¯ Unit Trust - Better Choice

Sarah can start with RM 1,000 in a balanced fund, keeping emergency money. Professional managers handle research and diversification. She can set up automatic monthly investments.

Scenario 2: Mid-Career Professional - RM 50,000 to Invest
Ahmad has RM 50,000 and enjoys analyzing businesses. He reads financial news regularly and wants more control over his investments. He has 10+ years until retirement.

ðŸ“ˆ Individual Stocks - Good Fit

Ahmad can build a diversified portfolio of 8-10 stocks across sectors. His knowledge and interest make stock picking suitable. He has enough capital to spread risk effectively.

ðŸŽ¯ Unit Trust - Also Good

Or combine both! Use 60% for unit trusts (core holdings) and 40% for individual stocks (satellite holdings). This balances professional management with personal control.

Scenario 3: Parent Saving for Child's Education - 10 Years Timeline
Fatimah wants to save RM 30,000 over 10 years for her child's university education. She needs steady growth with controlled risk and can't afford major losses.

ðŸ“ˆ Individual Stocks - Risky

Too risky for a fixed-timeline goal. If the market crashes near year 10, she might lose a significant portion. Stock picking requires expertise she may not have.

ðŸŽ¯ Unit Trust - Ideal Choice

Education-focused unit trust or balanced fund provides professional management, diversification, and risk management. Can set up monthly contributions (RM 250/month) with auto-debit.

Correct! Unit trusts provide instant diversification and professional management, making them ideal for beginners who lack time or expertise for individual stock research.

2. If you have RM 2,000 and want to invest in 5 different sectors, which is more practical?

Individual stocks - RM 400 per stock
Unit trust - already diversified across sectors

Correct! RM 2,000 is too small to effectively diversify across stocks (minimum 1 lot = 100 shares). A unit trust instantly provides diversification across multiple sectors and companies.

3. Which statement about fees is TRUE?

Correct! Stocks have brokerage fees per trade (0.1-0.4%), while unit trusts have annual management fees (0.5-2%). Which is "cheaper" depends on your trading frequency and amount invested.
